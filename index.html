About Me

I am a fifth-year PhD Candidate in Operations Research in the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, advised by Prof. Dimitris Bertsimas. My primary research interests lie at the intersection of Optimization and Machine Learning, with an emphasis on nonconvex optimization, fairness in machine learning and robust optimization, with applications to energy optimization. During my PhD I conducted research on several topics, including primarily the design of algorithms for nonconvex optimization problems. In terms of robust optimization, I developed a method for solving robust nonlinear optimization problems that cannot be solved by existing approaches. I also developed an optimization framework for constructing data-driven uncertainty sets, leveraging machine learning, and finally I addressed some of the fundamental linear algebra problems under data uncertainty. In terms of machine learning, I designed a method for learning classifiers that are fair in terms of a sensitive attribute in an interpretable way, as well as a method for generating synthetic data points, when learning classifiers from imbalanced data. In the summer of 2022 I interned at Amazon, under the group of Dr. Ivan Oliveira, where I had the opportunity to develop an integrated optimization-machine learning approach to deal with practical large-scale optimization problems. Before joining MIT, I received a Diploma-MEng in Applied Mathematical and Physical Sciences from the National Technical University of Athens.
